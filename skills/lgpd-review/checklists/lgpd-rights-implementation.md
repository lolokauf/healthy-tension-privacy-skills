# LGPD Data Subject Rights Implementation Checklist

Audit checklist for verifying implementation of the 9 data subject rights under LGPD Art. 18, plus the right to review automated decisions (Art. 20). For each right, check both existence (does a mechanism exist?) and functionality (does it work end-to-end?).

---

## 1. Confirmation of Processing (Art. 18(I))

Data subjects can request confirmation of whether their personal data is being processed.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Confirmation endpoint exists | `GET /api/privacy/processing-status`, `/api/user/data-check`, or equivalent | HIGH |
| Returns clear yes/no | Response confirms or denies processing of the requester's data | HIGH |
| Identity verification | Email verification or re-authentication to confirm requester identity | MEDIUM |
| Response timeline | Immediate for simplified format (Art. 19(I)), or complete statement within 15 days (Art. 19(II)) | HIGH |

---

## 2. Access to Data (Art. 18(II))

Data subjects can request access to their personal data.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Access endpoint exists | `GET /api/user/data`, `/api/privacy/access`, data export feature, or equivalent | HIGH |
| Returns all personal data | Response includes all data elements from Personal Data Classification | HIGH |
| Includes processing details | Response states purposes, categories of data, recipients/sharing | MEDIUM |
| Machine-readable format | Data provided in a structured, commonly used format (JSON, CSV, XML) | MEDIUM |
| Identity verification | Re-authentication or email verification | HIGH |
| Simplified format option | Art. 18(§3)(I) — immediate response for simplified requests | MEDIUM |

---

## 3. Correction (Art. 18(III))

Data subjects can request correction of incomplete, inaccurate, or outdated personal data.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Correction endpoint exists | `PATCH /api/user/profile`, `/api/privacy/correct`, or equivalent | HIGH |
| Accepts documented corrections | Data subject can specify what is inaccurate and provide correct information | MEDIUM |
| Propagates corrections | Corrections forwarded to processors (*operadores*) and third parties that received the data (Art. 18(§6)) | HIGH |
| Identity verification | Re-authentication or email verification | HIGH |

---

## 4. Anonymisation, Blocking, or Deletion of Unnecessary/Excessive Data (Art. 18(IV))

Data subjects can request anonymisation, blocking, or deletion of data that is unnecessary, excessive, or processed in violation of LGPD.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Anonymisation mechanism exists | Ability to irreversibly strip identifying attributes from specific data records | HIGH |
| Blocking mechanism exists | Ability to suspend processing of specific data while maintaining storage | MEDIUM |
| Deletion mechanism exists | Ability to delete specific data elements (not just full account deletion) | HIGH |
| Criteria assessment | Mechanism to determine whether data is "unnecessary" or "excessive" relative to stated purpose | MEDIUM |
| Propagation to processors | Anonymisation/blocking/deletion propagated to processors and third parties (Art. 18(§6)) | HIGH |
| Identity verification | Re-authentication | HIGH |

### Distinguishing from Art. 18(VI) Deletion

Art. 18(IV) applies to data processed on ANY legal basis that is unnecessary/excessive. Art. 18(VI) specifically applies to data processed on the basis of consent. Both require deletion capability, but Art. 18(IV) has a broader trigger.

---

## 5. Data Portability (Art. 18(V))

Data subjects can request portability of their data to another service provider or product.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Portability endpoint exists | `GET /api/user/export`, `/api/privacy/portability`, download feature, or equivalent | HIGH |
| Structured format | Data provided in machine-readable, structured, commonly used format | HIGH |
| Includes all portable data | Data the subject provided plus data generated by their activity (derived data portability is broader under LGPD than GDPR) | MEDIUM |
| Transfer capability | Mechanism to transfer data directly to another service provider (not just download) | LOW |
| Identity verification | Re-authentication | HIGH |
| Excludes trade secrets | Portability must respect commercial and industrial secrets (Art. 18(§5)) | MEDIUM |

---

## 6. Deletion of Consent-Based Data (Art. 18(VI))

Data subjects can request deletion of personal data processed on the basis of consent, even if the data itself is not unnecessary or excessive.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Deletion endpoint exists | `DELETE /api/user/data`, `/api/privacy/delete-consent-data`, or equivalent | HIGH |
| Identifies consent-based data | System can distinguish data processed under consent from data processed under other bases | HIGH |
| Deletes from all storage | Primary database, caches, backups (within reasonable timeframe), analytics, logs | HIGH |
| Notifies processors | Deletion propagated to processors and third parties that received the data (Art. 18(§6)) | HIGH |
| Identity verification | Re-authentication (heightened) | HIGH |
| Handles exceptions | Art. 16 specifies 4 situations where data may be retained despite deletion request | MEDIUM |

### Retention Exceptions (Art. 16)

Data may be retained despite a deletion request under Art. 18(VI) only when:
1. Compliance with a legal or regulatory obligation (Art. 16(I))
2. Research (anonymised when possible) (Art. 16(II))
3. Transfer to a third party (provided legal basis requirements are met) (Art. 16(III))
4. Exclusive use by the controller (anonymised, no third-party access) (Art. 16(IV))

---

## 7. Information About Third-Party Sharing (Art. 18(VII))

Data subjects can request information about which public and private entities their personal data has been shared with.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Sharing disclosure exists | Endpoint or page listing all third parties that receive the data subject's data | HIGH |
| Lists specific entities | Named vendors, not generic categories (e.g., "Stripe" not "payment processors") | MEDIUM |
| Includes purpose per entity | Why data is shared with each entity | MEDIUM |
| Kept up to date | Sharing list reflects current vendor relationships in code, not stale documentation | MEDIUM |
| Identity verification | Email verification or session auth | MEDIUM |

---

## 8. Information About Denying Consent (Art. 18(VIII))

Data subjects have the right to information about the possibility and consequences of denying consent.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Consequences disclosed | Consent forms/screens explain what happens if consent is denied | HIGH |
| Specific to each consent purpose | Each consent request explains its specific consequences, not generic "service may be limited" | MEDIUM |
| Shown before consent decision | Consequences visible at the time consent is requested, not only after denial | HIGH |
| No coercive language | Consequences described factually, not as threats or dark patterns (e.g., "you will lose access" vs "your experience will be degraded") | MEDIUM |

---

## 9. Consent Revocation (Art. 18(IX))

Data subjects can revoke previously given consent at any time, through a free and facilitated process.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Revocation mechanism exists | Settings toggle, `/api/privacy/revoke-consent`, unsubscribe link, or equivalent | CRITICAL |
| Equally accessible as consent | Revocation path is not more burdensome than the original consent mechanism (Art. 8(§5)) | HIGH |
| Granular revocation | Can revoke consent for specific purposes without revoking all consents | HIGH |
| Effective immediately | Processing based on revoked consent stops — no "processing will stop in 30 days" | HIGH |
| Prior processing valid | Revocation does not retroactively invalidate processing that occurred before revocation (Art. 8(§5)) | MEDIUM |
| Confirmation | Data subject notified that revocation was processed | LOW |

---

## Right to Review Automated Decisions (Art. 20)

Data subjects may request review of decisions made solely on the basis of automated processing that affect their interests, including decisions intended to define their personal, professional, consumer, or credit profile.

| Check | What to Look For | Severity if Missing |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Automated decision identification | System identifies which decisions are made solely by automated processing (ML models, rule engines, scoring algorithms) | HIGH |
| Review mechanism exists | Endpoint or process for requesting human review of an automated decision | HIGH |
| Explanation of criteria | Controller must provide clear and adequate information about the criteria and procedures used (Art. 20(§1)) | HIGH |
| Human review capability | Infrastructure exists for a human to review and potentially override the automated decision | HIGH |
| Audit trail | Automated decision inputs, model version, and output are logged for review purposes | MEDIUM |

### Scope of Art. 20

Art. 20 is broader than GDPR Art. 22 in some respects:
- Covers decisions that "affect interests" — not limited to decisions with "legal or similarly significant effects"
- Applies to profiling for personal, professional, consumer, or credit purposes — common in many applications
- The right to explanation (Art. 20(§1)) requires the controller to disclose criteria and procedures, which may conflict with trade secrets — the controller must balance transparency against commercial confidentiality

## Response Timeline Summary

| Request Type | Timeline | Reference |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Simplified confirmation of processing | Immediate | Art. 19(I) |
| Complete statement of data | Clear and complete, within 15 days from the request | Art. 19(II) |
| Correction, deletion, anonymisation | Immediate communication to processors and third parties | Art. 18(§6) |
| All other rights | Within reasonable timeframe — LGPD does not specify a fixed period for most rights | Art. 18 (general) |

**Note:** Unlike GDPR's firm 1-month timeline, LGPD uses "immediately" and "reasonable time" for most rights. Art. 19(II) specifies 15 days ("dias") from the request for complete statements — the statute does not say "business days" (*dias úteis*); the 15-business-day interpretation comes from ANPD administrative guidance. The obligation to notify processors and third parties about corrections, deletions, and anonymisation is immediate (Art. 18(§6)).

## Identity Verification Guidance

LGPD does not prescribe specific identity verification methods. The controller must use reasonable methods to confirm the requester's identity. Match verification stringency to the risk:

| Right | Risk Level | Suggested Verification |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|
| Confirmation of processing (Art. 18(I)) | Low | Email verification |
| Access to data (Art. 18(II)) | Medium | Re-authentication or email + security question |
| Correction (Art. 18(III)) | Medium | Re-authentication |
| Anonymisation/blocking/deletion (Art. 18(IV)) | High | Re-authentication + confirmation step |
| Portability (Art. 18(V)) | High | Re-authentication |
| Deletion of consent data (Art. 18(VI)) | High | Re-authentication + confirmation step |
| Sharing information (Art. 18(VII)) | Low | Session authentication |
| Consent denial info (Art. 18(VIII)) | None | Available without verification (informational) |
| Consent revocation (Art. 18(IX)) | Medium | Session authentication |
| Automated decision review (Art. 20) | Medium | Re-authentication |
